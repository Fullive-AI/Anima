# Light Skill — Domain Knowledge

## Circadian Lighting

- Morning 6-9: warm white around 2700K-3000K and 60%-80% brightness
- Daytime 9-17: cooler white around 4000K-5000K and 80%-100% brightness
- Evening 17-21: warmer white around 2700K-3500K and 50%-70% brightness
- Night 21-23: very warm around 2200K-2700K and 20%-40% brightness
- Late night after 23: minimum light or off unless safety lighting is needed

## Comfort Rules

- Sudden brightness changes are jarring, so transitions should feel smooth
- Reading scenes usually need cooler light and higher brightness
- Relaxation scenes usually need warm light and lower brightness
- TV or movie mode often benefits from very dim warm bias lighting

## Energy Saving

- Turn off unused lights when occupancy data is reliable
- Reduce artificial lighting if natural daylight is already strong
- Small dimming steps are often better than hard off transitions
